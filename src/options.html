FullTouch

Fullscreen touch controls for tablets and 2-in-1 laptops

No data collected. None. Ever. FullTouch has no servers and no analytics. It never stores or transmits the pages you visit - nothing leaves your device. Your settings below are saved only in your own Chrome.
Then why does Chrome warn it can “read and change all your data on all sites”?

That warning is mandatory for any extension allowed to run on every website - and FullTouch's gestures have to work everywhere you browse, so it has to ask. But “allowed to run on every site” is not “collects your data.” FullTouch only watches for touch gestures and draws its own nav bar on top of the page. It contains no networking code of any kind - no fetch, no tracking, no phone-home - so there is literally nothing in it that could send your data anywhere.

Tips

  • In fullscreen, swipe down from the very top edge to reveal the bar.
  • Tap anywhere off the bar, swipe up on the bar, or press Esc to hide it without leaving fullscreen.
  • Tap ✕ to leave fullscreen entirely.
  • Swipe two fingers sideways to switch tabs - right for the previous (left) tab, left for the next. Turn it off or reverse it in the Gesture section above.
  • Keyboard fallbacks (editable at chrome://extensions/shortcuts): Alt+N toggles the bar, Alt+Shift+F exits fullscreen.
